The word "aurora" denotes the beginning of a new day, new hope, a new era, and progress.

Aurora (Tagalog: Lalawigan ng Aurora; Ilocano: Probinsia ti Aurora)

is a province in the Philippines located in the eastern part of Central Luzon region, facing the Philippine Sea. Its capital is Baler and borders, clockwise from the south, the provinces of Quezon, Bulacan, Nueva Ecija, Nueva Vizcaya, Quirino, and Isabela.

Aurora is a coastal province covering an area of 3,147.32 s/k in east-central Luzon

Aurora bordered by the Northern Sierra Madre Natural Park of Isabela, to the west by the central range of the Sierra Madre which contains the Casecnan Protected Landscape and Aurora Memorial National Park, to the south by the Umiray River, and to the east by the Philippine Sea which opens to the Pacific Ocean. The San Ildefonso Peninsula lies in the province's northern portion between the Philippine Sea and the Casiguran Sound.

Topography and Climate

The province covers a portion of the Sierra Madre mountain range. As such, the elevation is generally steep to very steep and only about 14% of the province's total area is flat and Aurora's climate is classified as Tropical rainforest climate. It experiences significant rainfall throughout the year. Because the coastal province faces the Pacific Ocean, it is frequently visited by typhoons.

The 8 municipalities of the province comprise a total of 151 barangays, with Suclayin in Baler as the most populous in 2010, and Dibalo in San Luis as the least.

Ang bayan ng Aurora ay nahahati sa 33 mga barangay.


Aurora is politically subdivided into 8 municipalities, all encompassed by a lone legislative district.

Province of Aurora is the 73rd province of the Philippines created by Batas Pambasa Blg. 07. It is the youngest among the seven (7) provinces of Central Luzon.

Aurora was originally composed of the municipalities of Baler and Casiguran.
